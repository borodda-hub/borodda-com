Bridges was a conceptual proposal for a public artwork at urban scale: nearly 1,000 individually addressed lights placed in the Chicago River bed, mapped to a slice of the night sky and updated in real time. The work was never built — but as a proposal it sits inside the same line of inquiry as the later urban-computing projects, asking what a city's hidden infrastructure can be made to speak.
