(re)ACT staged a meeting between a conventional architectural surface — the Shoji screen, a translucent paper-and-wood divider with centuries of design history — and a real-time computational layer that registered viewer movement and rendered it back into the screen as projected pattern. The work was installed at Platform 01 in Beppu, Japan, and asked what happens when a familiar quiet object is suddenly responsive.
