City of Big Data Maps was a sequence of large-format visualization works produced for the Chicago Architecture Foundation's City of Big Data exhibition — including a 96-by-96-inch fabric print of Chicago open-data layers and a companion piece developed with the University of Illinois Chicago on neighborhood health. The works tested how analytic visualizations behave when scaled past the laptop screen and into a public exhibition register, where readers move physically across the map rather than scrolling through it.
